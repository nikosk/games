# Wild Pairs

**Existing version:** `animal-memory.html`

## Idea

A gentle wildlife matching game presented as a field journal. Match pairs, enjoy the animal illustrations and sounds, and gradually fill a collection.

**Player fantasy:** Explore nature and discover animals.

## Main play

1. Choose a small set of animal cards.
2. Turn over two cards.
3. Keep a match or remember where a mismatch was.
4. Finish the board and enjoy a short celebration.
5. Replay with another set or habitat.

Keep the current game's positive, pressure-free feel.

## Controls

| Action | Touch | Keyboard/mouse |
|---|---|---|
| Turn card | Tap card | Click card |
| Choose board or habitat | Tap | Click |
| Open collection | Tap journal | Click journal |
| Replay a sound | Tap animal | Click animal |

Cards need generous spacing and readable art on the target tablet.

## Current MVP

A procedural watercolour meadow field journal with selectable 2×4, 3×4, 4×4, and 6×6 boards (4, 6, 8, or 18 pairs), touch-friendly cards, match/mismatch feedback, generated Web Audio cues, counters, replay, fullscreen, and a completion celebration.

## If it stays fun

- more habitats and animal sets
- several board sizes
- a field album
- short animal facts for reading together
- saved discoveries
- habitat ambience

Do not commit to dozens of animals before one small set feels delightful.

## Look and sound

Watercolour wildlife cards, a warm paper journal, simple habitat colors, paper turns, soft match chimes, and recognizable animal sounds where good sources are practical.

## Tricky logic worth testing

- pair generation and shuffle
- blocking input while cards resolve
- match counting
- collection deduplication
- saved discoveries

## Ready for the portfolio when

A complete board is pleasant to play by touch, cards remain readable, the art and sound feel coherent, and replaying is inviting.
